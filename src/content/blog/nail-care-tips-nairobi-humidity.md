Nairobi's warm, humid climate can be challenging for even the most expertly applied polish. Here's how our clients keep their nails looking salon-fresh longer.

**1. Dehydrate the Nail Plate Thoroughly**

Humidity means moisture in the air — and on your nails. Our prep routine includes a thorough dehydration step with acetone to ensure zero oil or moisture remains before base coat application.

**2. Always Use a Base Coat**

This isn't optional. A quality base coat creates a barrier between your natural nail and the colour layer, preventing staining and improving adhesion.

**3. Seal the Tips**

The most common cause of chipping in humid weather is polish lifting at the free edge. We cap every nail tip with colour and top coat — extending the life of your manicure by 3–5 days.

**4. Reapply Top Coat Every 2–3 Days**

A fresh layer of top coat revives shine and reinforces the seal. We recommend our clients keep a mini bottle at their desk.

**5. Avoid Water for the First 12 Hours**

Water is fresh polish's worst enemy. After your manicure, try to keep your hands dry for at least half a day to allow full curing.

**6. Weekly Oil Massage**

Nairobi's alternating sun and rain can dry out cuticles. A daily drop of jojoba or vitamin E oil keeps the nail bed flexible and prevents breakage.
